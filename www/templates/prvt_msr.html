Floods

  • Know your risk: Identify flood-prone areas in your locality. Learn about the local flood history.
  • Prepare your home: Elevate electrical appliances and waterproof your basement. Install check valves in plumbing to prevent floodwater backflow. Clear gutters and drains regularly.
  • Create a family plan: Establish a meeting place and communication strategy. Teach children how to dial emergency numbers. Practice evacuation routes with your family.
  • Stay alert: Listen to weather forecasts and warnings. Have a battery-powered weather radio. Follow instructions from local authorities promptly.
  • Protect valuable items: Move valuable items to higher levels of your home. Store important documents in waterproof containers.
  • Get flood insurance: Check if you live in a flood zone and get appropriate insurance. Review your policy annually and understand what it covers.

Cyclones

  • Secure your home: Install storm shutters, reinforce doors, and roof. Trim trees and shrubs around your home. Secure outdoor objects that could become projectiles.
  • Create an emergency kit: Include food, water, medicines, and important documents. Add flashlights, batteries, and a first-aid kit. Include personal hygiene items and protective clothing.
  • Evacuate if advised: Follow local authorities' instructions. Plan multiple evacuation routes. Inform family or friends of your evacuation plans.
  • Stay informed: Monitor weather updates through reliable sources. Keep a battery-powered radio on hand. Sign up for local emergency alerts.
  • Prepare for power outages: Stock up on non-perishable food. Have a generator ready if possible. Ensure you have enough fuel for generators and vehicles.
  • Protect important documents: Store copies of personal documents in a waterproof container. Include identification, insurance policies, and medical records.

Droughts

  • Conserve water: Fix leaks, use water-saving fixtures, and limit lawn watering. Take shorter showers and turn off taps while brushing teeth.
  • Plan ahead: Develop a water usage plan and store emergency water supplies. Invest in rainwater harvesting systems. Use greywater systems for irrigation.
  • Plant drought-resistant plants: Use native vegetation that requires less water. Implement xeriscaping techniques.
  • Stay informed: Follow updates from local water authorities. Participate in community water conservation programs.
  • Use efficient irrigation: Water plants during cooler parts of the day. Use drip irrigation systems to minimize water waste.
  • Educate your community: Raise awareness about water conservation methods. Encourage community-wide water-saving initiatives.
